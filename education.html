My Life

"Negroes in this country--and Negroes do not, strictly or legally speaking, exist in any other--are taught really to despise themselves from the moment their eyes open on the world. This world is white and they're black." (James Baldwin, "Down at the Cross: A Letter from a Region in My Mind (1962)" 35)

My Life

1975-1993

Baltimore, MD

My Upbringing and Perspective
  • Lived the fear of violence and prejudice of Black People first hand
  • Learned a fundamental understanding of fighting for equality
  • Exposed to education, from a mother who specialized in education, and activism, with a father involved with the Black Panther Party
  • Gained a unique perspective with a family of a multide of childern who are children to four different woman
    • 1975

    Exposure to Activism

    Shaping my Perception of the World Around Me
  • Black Nationalism: the idea of equality, independence, and self sufficency for black people
  • Black Nationalists such as Malcom X
  • Challenging the idea of the polarization of color, a boundary between 'black' and 'white.' and people who argued this, such as James Baldwin
  • Athlete of the Year Award
    • 1992

    Expression Through Writing

    My Intrinsic and True Form of Expression
  • "I grew up in a house full of books [...] it was the thing I displayed some of my talents [...] I started writing poetry when I was 17" (Alter)
  • “Poetry was the processing of my thoughts until the slag of justification fell away, and I was left with the cold steel truths of life.” (Coates, "Between the World and Me" 52)
    • 1993-1999

    Howard University

    Publicist

    Publicist and Reporter for a Multitude of Papers
  • Worked for The Washington City Paper, The Village Voice, Philadelphia Weekly, and Time
  • Aided my writing ability and helped me refine my unique style
  • Prepared me for my shift to more important positions as a commentator and essayist
    • 2008-2018

    The Atlantic

    National Correspondant
  • Gained a wide supporting base and published extremly influencial works
  • "Here we find the roots of American wealth and democracy—in the for-profit destruction of the most important asset available to any people, the family. The destruction was not incidental to America’s rise; it facilitated that rise. By erecting a slave society, America created the economic foundation for its great experiment in democracy." (Coates, "The Case for Reperations)

    • 2015-pres

    BTWAM

    Between the World and Me: Most famous and recognized work
  • Won the National Book Award for Nonfiction
  • Earned critical acclaim and recognition
  • Expanded my influence and ability to make change in the world
  • Emphasized the realities of society, "It wasn’t a book. There was no story, no long-form anything. It’s one of the first things I’ve written where it came out of a feeling. There was no arc." (Alter)
